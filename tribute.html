CRISTIANO RONALDO

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro (Born 5 February 1985, Currently 37) is a Portuguese professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Manchester United and captains the Portugal national team. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, Ronaldo has won five Ballon d'Or awards[note 3] and four European Golden Shoes, the most by a European player. He has won 32 trophies in his career, including seven league titles, five UEFA Champions Leagues, and the UEFA European Championship. Ronaldo holds the records for most appearances (183), goals (140), and assists (42) in the Champions League, goals in the European Championship (14), international goals (117), and international appearances by a European (191). He is one of the few players to have made over 1,100 professional career appearances, and has scored over 800 official senior career goals for club and country. He his also the most followed individual on instagram with 493 Million Followers.

Early Life

Cristiano Ronaldo dos Santos Aveiro was born in the São Pedro parish of Funchal, the capital of the Portuguese island of Madeira, and grew up in the nearby parish of Santo António. He is the fourth and youngest child of Maria Dolores dos Santos Viveiros da Aveiro, a cook, and José Dinis Aveiro, a municipal gardener and part-time kit man. Ronaldo grew up in an impoverished Catholic Christian home, sharing a room with all his siblings.

Club career

SPORTING CP(2002-2003)
On 29 September 2002, Ronaldo made his debut in the Primeira Liga, against Braga and on 7 October, he scored two goals against Moreirense in their 3–0 win.
Manchester United manager Alex Ferguson was determined to acquire Ronaldo on a permanent move urgently, after Sporting defeated United 3–1 at the inauguration of the Estádio José Alvalade in August 2003.
After the game, Ferguson agreed to pay Sporting £12.24 million[26] for what he considered to be "one of the most exciting young players" he had ever seen.
MANCHESTER UNITED(2003-2009)
Ronaldo's move to Manchester United was completed on 12 August 2003. His debut however was not until the opening fixture of the 2003/04 league.
Although he requested the number 28, his number at Sporting, he received the squad number 7 shirt, which had previously been worn by such United players as George Best, Eric Cantona and David Beckham.[32] Wearing the number 7 became an extra source of motivation for Ronaldo. During his time at United he had 84 Goals, 34 assists in 196 appearances
Aged 18, winning the FA Cup in his first season. He would also go on to win three consecutive Premier League titles, the Champions League and the FIFA Club World Cup; at age 23, he won his first Ballon d'Or
REAL MADRID CF(2009-2018)
Ahead of the 2009–10 season, Ronaldo joined Real Madrid for a world record transfer fee at the time, of £80 million (€94 million)
During his time in Real Madrid he broke several records(many of these were in the champions league, hence his nickname "Mr.Champions League") which would go on to solidify him as truly one of the greatest players of all time
He had 450 goals, 131 assists in 438 appearances in all competitions
He also won 4 Champions Leagues, 4 Ballon d'ors, 3 League Titles, 3 Golden boots and 2 League-cups
JUVENTUS (2018-2021)
Despite months of negotiation to sign a new Real Madrid contract,[225] on 10 July 2018, Ronaldo signed a four-year contract with Italian club Juventus after completing a €100 million transfer
He Scored 101 goals and had 22 assists in 134 appearances
MANCHESTER UNITED(2021-)
On 27 August 2021, Manchester United announced they had reached an agreement with Juventus to re-sign Ronaldo, subject to agreement of personal terms, visa and medical
He has since scored 27 goals and had 4 assists in 57 appearances

International Career

Ronaldo began his international career with Portugal under-15 in 2001. On February 6, 2007 he was assigned captain for the first time and given the number 7 shirt.
As of now, he has the most international goals of any player with a tally of 135 goals.
